(01) — Services

Not a catalog, not fixed packages. Four ways to solve operational problems with technology. In every one, the starting point is your real process, not a pre-made product.

1.

Platforms built from scratch

Tailored software
example — platform

Technology: modern web applications (TypeScript), hosted on European cloud infrastructure.

a.

What it is

Platforms and management systems designed from scratch for your company's processes. The software adapts to the operation, never the other way around.

b.

Signs you need this

  • You run the operation on spreadsheets (or other outdated tools) that only one person understands.
  • The off-the-shelf software you bought forces the team into workarounds and patches.
  • You have a unique process that no off-the-shelf product covers.
c.

Concrete examples

  • Reporting platform for financial services: data from multiple sources consolidated, with per-client reports.
  • Hotel back-office: invoice capture by photo, cost control and automatic monthly P&L.
  • Internal portal for managing requests between departments.
d.

What we deliver

  • A web app accessible from any device.
  • User manual and team training.
  • Code and documentation, owned by the client.
2.

Less manual work

Process automation
example — automation

Technology: automations and workers on European cloud infrastructure, with monitoring and failure alerts.

a.

What it is

We identify the most costly repetitive manual tasks in your operation and put software to do them: faster, with far fewer errors, at any hour.

b.

Signs you need this

  • Reports that take a whole day to put together.
  • Routine emails written one by one.
  • Checks and reconciliations done line by line.
c.

Concrete examples

  • Automatic generation of monthly management reports.
  • Reconciliation of bank transactions against invoicing, with discrepancies flagged.
  • Automatic detection of duplicate invoices before payment.
d.

What we deliver

  • An automated process in production.
  • Alerts when something fails.
  • Measurement of time saved.
3.

Connecting what you already use

Integrations & modules
example — integration

Technology: APIs, webhooks and custom connectors; European infrastructure.

a.

What it is

Pieces of software that connect the systems you already use and fill the gaps between them. Nothing gets replaced, no painful migration.

b.

Signs you need this

  • Someone on the team copies data from one program to another every week.
  • Your ERP or CRM handles 80% of what you need, and the other 20% is done by hand.
  • You want reports that combine data from two systems that don't talk to each other.
c.

Concrete examples

  • Connection between billing software and a treasury control spreadsheet.
  • Daily import of transactions and positions from banking platforms for reporting.
  • Automatic exports to the accountant in the right format.
d.

What we deliver

  • A working module.
  • Integration documentation.
  • Error monitoring.
4.

AI for real tasks

Applied AI
real screenshot — AI invoice reading

Technology: recent AI models; data handled in compliance with GDPR.

a.

What it is

We apply advanced AI models to concrete tasks in your operation. No surface-level chatbots: AI doing work that today is done by people, hour after hour.

b.

Signs you need this

  • The team spends hours reading documents and entering data into systems.
  • You receive information in messy formats (PDFs, emails, photos) that someone has to sort out.
  • You want quick answers about company data without waiting for reports.
c.

Concrete examples

  • Automatic reading of supplier invoices from photos, extracting NIF (Portuguese tax ID), amounts and supplier classification.
  • Reading client documents at onboarding (KYC): data extracted and verified automatically.
  • Automatic classification and routing of requests received by email.
d.

What we deliver

  • An AI workflow in production, with human validation where it makes sense.
  • Accuracy measurement.
  • Transparent usage costs.
